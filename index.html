Plane Boarding Simulation

Customizable simulation to find the efficiency of different boarding methods.

Purpose

Reducing plane boarding times by just 10 minutes has been estimated to save airlines over 160 million dollars a year, while improving passenger experience. The standard method of boarding a plane has been shown to be highly inefficient in computer simulations and physical studies. The purpose of this project was to create a realistic computer simulation of passengers boarding a plane to identify which boarding method was the fastest. 4 boarding methods were tested - Free-for-All (passengers with assigned seats in random order), WILMA (window, middle, then aisle), Standard (back to front after first class), and Back-to-Front. Unlike previous studies, this project added real life scenarios like luggage stowing delays, seat blockage, and slow passengers into the computer simulation simultaneously.
